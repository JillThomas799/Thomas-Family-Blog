Saturday, January 6th 2024
Harry and Bernard return to Penarth, and Jill and Elena have an icy adventure in the Lowther Valley.

Today it was time for Harry's return to Wales for the final few months of his PhD. Elena and Jill were staying back in Cumbria to look after Lennie, so the decision was made to drive over to Bampton, a tiny village situated in the Lowther valley between Shap and Haweswater, and to attempt a walk up Knipe Scar. The day did not start well as there was low-lying icy fog. Nonetheless, not to be deterred, Elena and Jill set off in the hope the fog would be lifting by the time we reached our destination of Bampton.

After a proficient piece of driving from Elena, who managed to return the car effectively to its original course when we skidded on some undetected black ice, we arrived in the Lowther Valley to find the mist was still low-lying. What else were we to do, but retire to Lowther café for drinks and cakes? We thought it would be quiet, but it seemed that many people had had the same idea!

As we left the café to once again try our luck down in the valley for the walk, what should we see but a fogbow which, as this link to the met office site explains, is similar in some respects to a traditional rainbow forming from sunlight interacting with water droplets contained in fog, mist or cloud rather than interacting with raindrops as it does in a classical rainbow.We had never seen a fogbow before, and it was quite spectacular.

We departed Lowther Castle and headed down the Lowther valley but then a very strange noise started to emanate from the brakes! It sounded as it one/ multiple of the brake pads might be rubbing.The lane was more or less single track with nowhere to stop, so we had no choice but to drive the 5 miles into Bampton where we could find parking. On arrival at the car park, there was no possibility of phoning for assistance as there was no mobile signal, and the fog was even thicker so no walk...

The decision was made to return to Elena's at Penrith and walk into Penrith town, which we did, returning home later in the day only to find...the fog was even thicker at home. So we can successfully say it was a thwarted trip, but with some spectacular scenery and the all-important café stop en route.
