February 2024

1st - 28th February 2024
Welcome to our February blog. This month we welcome our two star guests, our friends Robin and Cathy's dogs Wilf (the smaller dog on the right of the photo) and Nell (the larger dog on the left of the photo) , who accompanied us on our walk at Ambleside this month, so we have given Wilf and Nell star places in this month's blog.

To reflect the fact we have still been having some snow showers, we have stayed with the snow theme, albeit with a lighter fall. However, the snowman unfortunately has melted...

Do not worry though, Lennie has not been entirely left out, he has made an appearance in animated form at the bottom of the page...
Saturday, February 3rd 2024
Ikea Pax wardrobe arrives and is constructed at Elena's

Today the Ikea construction team turned up promptly at 8am to construct her PAX wardrobe, which had arrived the previous day in flat pack format. The team consisted of one man with a staple gun and some efficient power tools and within 2 hours the wardrobe was constructed and safely attached to the wall as per the pictures below. We have decided IKEA are on to a very good marketing strategy offering this service....
Sunday, February 4th 2024
Trip to Acorn Bank

Trip to Acorn Bank, which is owned by the National Trust and particularly known for its herbs and fruit orchards. It is not usually open before Easter, so we were pleased to discover that the National Trust works closely with the local village of Culgaith, whose residents had asked whether the National Trust would consider opening the property earlier than usual so they could see the snow drops in the grounds, which are very pretty at the moment.

It was an excellent opportunity for Elena to sign up for National Trust membership, which once the annual membership is paid, allows her to access National Trust properties in England, Wales, Northern Ireland and Scotland and also to obtain free parking on the many National Trust car parks in Cumbria, especially in the Lake District National Park.

The snow drops were proving quite popular as there were quite a lot of people there. The beds of snow drops were lovely and whilst some areas of the site were inaccessible as a result of the recent poor weather eg. the mill was closed off and the more extended walk to the village via the fields was inaccessible due to thick mud, otherwise everything was fine. The National Trust had wisely opened the outdoor refreshment stall, and even better had opened the indoor café seating area to sit inside out of the cold. Once in the café, Bernard was trying maintain his diet, but kept trying to take some of Elena's gingerbread!
Wednesday, February 7th 2024
Trip over to Ambleside to meet up with friends

It was great today to meet up in Ambleside with some university friends whom we have known for many years. We went on a lovely walk from Ambleside, initially passing the memorial of flowers and tributes left to Riley , a well-known Ambleside cat who died on the 23rd January 2024 when he was hit by a car. A fund has been set up to create a bronze statue of him. We started off in the centre of town and headed initially across the park before picking up the bridle way towards Loughrigg Fell just before Brow Head Farm. Following the climb up around Miller Brow and beyond, we then dropped down the back of Loughrigg until we reached the very scenic Loughrigg Tarn, with a superb view of the Langdale Pikes behind.

After skirting around the tarn, we took a left turn on meeting the road again to drop down into Skelwith Bridge and a well-earned break at Chester's café, before returning on a small back-road to Ambleside to complete the 9 mile walk.
Saturday, February 10th 2024
Hexham for lunch

Bernard set off today for Cardiff, ready to help with Harry's move back to Cumbria on Thursday. Elena had been very busy at work all week, so Jill and Elena decided a trip out to Hexham was called for, with lunch at Le Bouchon French Restaurant.

It was as well we had arrived early as it was the day of the twice-monthly Hexham Farmer's Market, where producers from within a 50 mile radius come to sell their produce which includes locally reared meat and locally grown vegetables and fruits (see the photos below). It is always very popular.

Elena also took the opportunity to buy some tester paints, to try out the colours for the feature walls in her house...

Saturday, February 10th - Friday February 16th 2024
Harry bids a fond final farewell to Cardiff (at least for the time being)...apart from of course the final viva...and the PhD ceremony... and any future visits......

Harry is due to finish his PhD on the 31st March 2024 and is now undertaking the final write up of his thesis. For this he will not have to be in Cardiff any longer and the tenancy on his flat is coming to an end, so it has been time for a final fond farewell to Cardiff (well,sort of...at least for the time being) and the move 320 miles or so up to the North, not least to reunite with Lennie, who meanwhile in anticipation, has been seriously misbehaving...

The removal company Intercity Removals were great and attended promptly to pack everything into the van. We would definitely give them 5* and recommend them. The journey up to Cumbria went as well as could be expected given it is a long way on very busy roads. The removal men had had the right idea, in breaking the journey overnight such they were at our home in Cumbria just after 9am the following morning!

But now is not the time to be sad, as it is the start of a new and exciting era for Harry, as he moves on to his next job ... First though it is time for him to have a well-deserved rest after all his hard work and whilst he decides where his future path will lie...
