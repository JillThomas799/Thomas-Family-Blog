Saturday, December 2nd, 2023
Trip to Carlisle on the train with Elena to go shopping and see the Christmas Market

It was the first weekend of the Christmas market in Carlisle today, so it was agreed we should catch the train into the city centre to do some shopping, visit the famous Bookends book shop, try out the popular Carlisle Cathedral café and then return home later in the afternoon.

It was snowing as we walked to the station and the cows in the field looked very cold. The station was deserted today, however when the train arrived it was busy, with a number of people wearing santa and reindeer antler hats. Quite a few people looked like they were going into see the Christmas market and some others were obviously traveling further afield. Everyone seemed in good spirits.

The bookshop had the new novel by Adrian Goldsworthy The Wall, which Jill bought. Carlisle Cathedral Café was very busy but had a good, reasonably-priced menu (see photo). There was also turkey, cranberry and stuffing sandwich as sandwich of the day, which Elena chose. Afterwards, it was time to visit the Christmas market, including looking at the German sausage stall, bringing back memories of our time living in Germany 5 years ago now.

Later, after returning home, it was time to put the christmas tree and decorations up in the living room. Needless to say Lennie the cat was not overly impressed with the tree...
Saturday, December 16th, 2023
Trip to Keswick for the day

Decided to go to Keswick for the day today, as the weather forecast was better than for the following few days. Keswick is always a good outing with some lovely walking for all skill levels and great shops and places to eat.

We parked up past the football ground and walked into town alongside the park. It was very busy as the Christmas market was on all day.

We had a lovely walk out to Friar's Crag and down the left side of Derwentwater, heading towards Walla Crag. Tha National Trust have done a lot of work improving all these footpaths. It was relatively quiet on the walk, as most people seemed to be in town shopping, rather than walking down the lake. There were some very atmospheric views down the lake, looking into the stormy weather.

Soon however, the weather took a turn for the worse, and it was time to return to town and go to see the Christmas markets.

Saturday, December 30th, 2023
ANNUAL TRIP OUT FOR THE FAMILY CHRISTMAS MEAL

This year, we decided to go somewhere new for the annual family Christmas meal out, and had dinner booked at the Kirkstyle Inn, Slaggyford which the food critic Grace Dent reviewed in the Guardian in July 2023. This was a long awaited trip as tables were much in demand.

The restaurant is set in a rural location in the North Pennines Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty (AONB), surrounded by the grouse moors. In the daytime, the scenery and views going out there are wild and spectacular but, being Winter, it was dark and very rainy so all eyes needed to be on the road. The inn is tucked away down a quiet country lane and has been in existence since the 18th century, when it was thought to have been a rectory, and afterwards, since mid 19th century, as an inn. All their food is sourced within a 50 mile radius and the dishes are prepared on site. They have their own food processing plant for preparing the game and ageing the meat.

The highlights of this inn are the quality of the food, particularly the game, but above all the ambience which is something quite unique and distinctive.
Firstly, there was the attempt to obtain some good family photos, to remind us in times to come of our dinner. However, this quickly turned in to a scenario similar to shooting a difficult film script, with many, many retakes...
And then, it was on to the meal itself ...
