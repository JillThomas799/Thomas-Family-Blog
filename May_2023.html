Monday, May 15th, 2023
An update on the garden today as the weather is gradually improving and it is becoming a little warmer,after the recent cold spell, so the plants are coming on a bit now.We have created 2 new raised beds and there are more to come yet. Bernard has commenced and has been working on 'Project Gravel' which involves section by lifting and cleaning of the gravelled outdoor areas, laying weed-control membrane and then replacing the gravel. It also involved the purchase of a large quantity of new gravel for the front drive to improve the appearance at the front.
In terms of the productive garden/allotment, the first new raised bed contains lettuce, beetroot and radishes.The second raised bed contains rhubarb, peas, leeks, radishes and parsley. There are also 2 apple trees and a plum tree. In the greenhouse are tomatoes and peppers and the seedlings being propagated.
Wednesday, May 24th, 2023
Thank you for all suggestions and feedback we have received on the blog. It is helpful to learn how people have got on when they have been accessing the blog on varying devices. As a result of this feedback, we will be looking at enabling enlargement and resetting of photo images by clicking on them as is common on websites displaying images over the next couple of weeks. An update on this will be along soon.
Also, the garden is continuing to come into bloom with the gorse in particular splendid this year after heavy pruning last year. We have also put in place a trellis against the wall as you ascend the steps to the terraces and have planted a climbing rose there in a pot. Other improvements have been some refashioning of the border to the left of the steps where 2 buddleia plants and a photinia for winter colour have been added to the bush rose, dianthus and blue and white campanula plants already in that border.
