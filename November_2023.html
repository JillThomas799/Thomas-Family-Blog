Trip to visit relatives in Essex:

Wednesday, 1st November: Day 3 - Visit to Audley End House and Gardens, Saffron Walden, Essex

Audley End House and Gardens are under the care of English Heritage and the house was one of the largest and most opulent houses in Jacobean England. The grounds were designed by Capability Brown, though not without disagreements, as the information boards point out. The parterre and organic kitchen gardens have been restored.
Trip to visit relatives in Essex:

Thursday, 2nd November: Day 4 - Trip to London to visit the National Gallery and to go to see English National Opera at the London Coliseum

A busy day today as it was off to London on the train to see the National Gallery and to go and see La Traviata performed by the English National Opera at the London Colisseum.

First, it was to the train station to catch the train into London. Then on to the National Gallery, which was extremely busy as it was half term week holiday for some of the schools. It was a good opportunity to see Turner's painting 'The Fighting Temeraire' which is housed at the gallery, amongst many other well-known paintings. We appeared to be not the only ones to find the gallery very large and busy as we met another gentleman asking us if we knew the way out as he had been wandering around some time trying to find it!

Then it was onto our pre-theatre dinner at Brown's Covent Garden before going on to the performance, which was very good.

Trip to visit relatives in Essex:

Friday, 3rd November: Day 5 - Return home via Leicestershire

It was a long journey for the return home today, but thankfully, we had the opportunity to break the journey en route to have a very enjoyable lunch with some friends in Leicestershire before arriving back home at 22.30.
Friday, 10th November, 2023
We have senior railcards!!! Our first trip on the train to Carlisle:

We now both qualify for senior railcards (isn't that an awful thought.....). However, there is a far more positive side, as after paying a small sum of money, we are now the proud owners of 2 senior railcards which entitle us to a third off the price of many, many rail fares. To try this out, we walked 23 minutes through the countryside to our local station and took the train into Carlisle. It is a scenic ride and all went very well. Plans for future trips include Newcastle, Hexham, Gretna, Glasgow, Edinburgh, Morpeth, Leeds, London and Durham and lots more. Also with the walks to and from the station each way, lots of exercise and cheaper and more eco-concious than taking the car!
